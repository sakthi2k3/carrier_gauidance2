The requirements for opting a technical field in engineering may vary depending on the country and the specific engineering field you want to pursue. However, some common requirements are:

Educational qualifications: You need to have completed your high school education with a strong background in science subjects, including physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

Entrance examination: Most universities or institutions require you to appear for an entrance exam, such as JEE Main, JEE Advanced, or other national-level or state-level engineering entrance exams.

Minimum age: Some universities or institutions have a minimum age requirement for applicants, which may be around 17-18 years.

Language proficiency: Some universities or institutions may require you to demonstrate proficiency in the language of instruction.

Good health: As with any technical field, you need to have good physical and mental health to cope with the demands of the course and profession.

Practical skills: Engineering is a practical field, and you need to have good problem-solving skills, analytical skills, and practical skills to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world problems.

Specific skills: Depending on the specific engineering field you want to pursue, there may be additional requirements. For example, if you want to pursue mechanical engineering, you may need to have a good understanding of physics and mechanics.
